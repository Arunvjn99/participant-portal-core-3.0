Redesign the Retirement Plan Review screen to improve hierarchy, reduce scrolling, and create a stronger confirmation moment before enrollment.

The current design presents the plan information as a vertical list of cards which requires excessive scrolling and does not highlight the final retirement outcome clearly.

The goal is to shift the page structure from a form-style summary to an outcome-first confirmation experience.

---

SECTION 1 — RETIREMENT OUTCOME (HERO)

Move the projected retirement balance to the top of the page as the primary visual element.

Display:

Projected retirement balance
$1.7M

Add supporting metrics in a compact grid below the main value:

Your yearly contribution
Employer match
Expected growth
Time horizon

This card should visually emphasize the projected retirement outcome and reinforce the value of the user’s plan.

---

SECTION 2 — PLAN SUMMARY GRID

Replace the stacked summary cards with a compact grid labeled:

Your Plan Setup

Display the following items in a responsive grid layout.

Plan
Contribution
Contribution source
Auto increase
Investment strategy

Each item should display its value and include an inline edit action.

Example:

Contribution
8% ($6,800/year)

Contribution Source
Pre-tax 100%

Investment Strategy
Balanced Portfolio

Use a two-column layout on mobile and a three-column layout on desktop to minimize vertical scrolling.

---

SECTION 3 — CONFIDENCE REINFORCEMENT

Add a small insight card reinforcing the value of the user’s plan.

Example messages:

Your employer contributes $5,100 per year to your retirement savings.

or

You are on track for retirement at age 65 with your current plan setup.

---

SECTION 4 — FINAL CONFIRMATION

Place the confirmation section directly below the summary.

Include:

Checkbox confirming retirement plan enrollment.

Example text:

I confirm my retirement plan enrollment and understand my contributions will begin next pay period.

Provide a link to view the full retirement plan terms.

---

PRIMARY ACTION

Replace the existing CTA with a stronger confirmation action.

Button label:

Enroll in Retirement Plan

---

UX GOAL

The review screen should reinforce confidence in the user’s retirement plan by highlighting the projected outcome first, presenting plan decisions in a compact and easily scannable layout, and minimizing scrolling on both mobile and desktop devices.
