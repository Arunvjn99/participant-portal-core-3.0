Refine the Investment Strategy screen by combining the strengths of the previous design and the new simplified layout.

The goal is to maintain a clean user experience while restoring important context and trust signals.

---

RISK PROFILE CONTEXT

At the top of the screen display the user’s previously determined risk profile.

Example:

You belong to: Aggressive Investor

Include a small edit option:

Change risk profile

This reassures the user that the investment recommendation is based on their earlier answers.

---

RECOMMENDED PORTFOLIO SECTION

Display a large recommended portfolio card.

Include:

Portfolio allocation chart

Example allocation:

US Stocks – 50%
International Stocks – 20%
Bonds – 20%
Real Estate – 10%

Include a primary CTA:

Use Recommended Portfolio

---

FUND TRANSPARENCY

Add a secondary action:

View funds included

When expanded, show the specific funds used in the recommended portfolio.

Example:

Vanguard Total Stock Market
Vanguard Total Bond Market
International Growth Fund

This improves transparency and trust.

---

CUSTOMIZATION OPTION

Below the recommended portfolio card include a clear secondary option:

Customize My Portfolio

This allows advanced users to manually choose funds and allocations.

Customization should not be the default path.

---

ALLOCATION SUMMARY PANEL

On the right side display an Allocation Summary card.

Include:

Portfolio allocation chart

List of selected funds with percentage allocations.

Example:

Vanguard Total Stock – 59.5%
Vanguard Total Bond – 19.5%
International Growth – 12%
Target Retirement 2060 – 9%

---

REMOVE REDUNDANT ELEMENTS

Remove duplicate advisor assistance sections.

Keep the interface focused on the investment decision.

---

FINAL RESULT

The investment step should feel:

Simple for beginners
Transparent for curious users
Flexible for advanced investors

The recommended portfolio should remain the primary action while customization remains optional.
