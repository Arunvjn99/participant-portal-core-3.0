Refine the Retirement Readiness screen to improve clarity, actionability, and emotional understanding of the readiness score.

The current design shows a readiness score and several numbers but does not clearly explain what the score means or how users can improve it.

The goal is to turn the screen into a clear financial progress indicator rather than a static analytics report.

---

READINESS SCORE CONTEXT

Keep the readiness score visualization but add explanatory text directly below it.

Example:

You are 57% on track for your retirement goal.

Add a benchmark reference:

Most participants your age aim for a readiness score of 65 or higher.

This helps users understand whether their score is good or needs improvement.

---

CONNECT SCORE TO OUTCOME

Place the projected retirement value closer to the readiness score.

Example:

Projected retirement balance
$459,000

This should appear directly below the readiness score to reinforce the relationship between the score and financial outcome.

---

ADD SCORE DRIVERS

Introduce a section explaining what factors influence the readiness score.

Example:

What impacts your readiness

Contribution rate
Employer match
Investment growth
Time horizon

Display these as small indicators to help users understand how their choices affect the score.

---

IMPROVE AI INSIGHT SECTION

Convert the AI insight into an actionable improvement card.

Example:

Increase your contribution by 2%

New readiness score: 71
Additional retirement savings: +$1,700/year

Provide an action button:

Apply change

---

ADD IMPROVEMENT OPTIONS

Include a short list of ways users can improve their readiness score.

Example:

Increase contribution by 2% → +14 readiness score
Enable auto increase → +9 readiness score
Delay retirement by 2 years → +11 readiness score

This allows users to see different paths to improvement.

---

INFORMATION HIERARCHY

The page structure should follow this order:

Readiness Score
Score Meaning
Projected Retirement Outcome
Ways to Improve
Contribution Metrics
Next Step

---

PRIMARY ACTION

Keep the main action:

Continue to Review

This confirms the user's setup and proceeds to the final confirmation step.

---

UX GOAL

The readiness screen should feel like a financial health check that helps users understand:

How prepared they are for retirement
What their projected outcome looks like
What actions could improve their future savings
